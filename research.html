Research

Small guys, disrupt PPIs.

Evaluation of small molecule peptidomimetics that could regulate key protein interactions based on molecular dynamics and data mining

Small molecule mimics of neurotrophins for eye disease

Neurotrophins are cytokines that interact with Trk receptors ceasing growth and survival. Unfortunately, they also interact with the “death receptor” p75 that cause apoptosis. In the eye, the growth and survival effects lead to secretion of a mucous that counteracts dryness. We are have made neurotrophins mimics that do not bind p75 yet do lead to secretion of that mucus. Such compounds are potential treatments for dry-eye disease and other maladies of the eye.

Fluor stapling, and capping.

We are investigating helices stabilized in novel ways, including via fluorescent dye straps, as probes and therapeutic leads, including for COVID-19, autophagy, and cancer.

Hows and whys, of tumor-seeking dyes.

We have discovered that cyanine-7 dyes that localized and persist in the tumor tend to do so because they form covalent albumin adducts in vivo. We are looking at the implications of this for diagnosis, imaging, and treatment of cancer via drug delivery.
